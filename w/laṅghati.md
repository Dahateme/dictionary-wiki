1. to jump over (acc.) step over, to hop Ja.iii.272; Ja.v.472 (langhamāno yāti); Mil.85.
2. to make light of, disregard neglect, transgress Pv\-a.15; Vv\-a.138  
   ■ Cp. *abhilaṅghati, ullaṅghati*

caus *laṅgheti (= laṅghati)* to jump over (acc.), lit. to make jump Ja.v.472 (vatiṃ) Thig.384 (Meruṃ langhetuṃ icchasi); Mil.85  
ger *laṅghayitvā* Thag\-a.255, & (poet.); *laṅghayitvāna* Ja.i.431 (= attānaṃ langhitvā C.); Mhvs.25, Mhvs.44 (pākāraṃ). Cp. *[olaṅgheti](olaṅgheti.md)*.

*laṅgh*, a by\-form of *lagh*, as in laghu (see *[lahu](lahu.md)*) light, quick; Idg. *\*legh & \*leṅgh;*, with meanings of both “quick” & “light” (or “little”) from the movement of jumping. Here belong Gr. ελαξύς little ἐλαφρός quick; Lat. levis (fr. \*leghṷis), Goth. leihto E. light; Ohg. lungar quick, Ger. ge\-lingen to succeed Further Lat. limen threshold. Perhaps also the words for “lungs,” viz. Ger. lunge, E. lights etc  
■ The Dhtp.33 defines *lagh (laṅgh)* by “gati\-sosanesu”
