Vatthu1

neuter lit. “ground,” hence

1. (lit.) object, real thing, property, thing, substance (cp. vatthu2!) AN.ii.209 (khetta˚, where khetta in lit. sense, cp. No. 2). Here belongs the defn of kāma as twofold: *vatthu\-kāma* and *kilesa\-kāma*, or desire for realities, objective kāma, and desire as property of stained character, i.e. subjective kāma, e.g. Mnd.1; Snp\-a.99, Snp\-a.112; Dhs\-a.62  
   ■ On *[vatthu](vatthu.md)* as general philosophical term cp. Dhs. translation §§ 455, 679, 1229, also introd. p. 86 *Cpd.* 15, 31, 1741.
2. (applied meaning) object, item Vin.i.121 (antima\-vatthuṃ ajjhāpannaka guilty of an extreme offence?); Vin.v.138 (the 10 āghāta\-vatthūni, as at Vb.86); DN.iii.252 (*seven* niddesa˚), DN.iii.255 (*eight* kusīta˚), DN.iii.258 (*eight* dāna˚); SN.ii.41, SN.ii.56 sq.; Vb.71 (cakkhu˚ etc.), Vb.306 sq., Vb.353; Ne.114 *(ten);* Snp\-a.172; Dhp\-a.iv.2 (akkosa˚); Pv\-a.8, Pv\-a.20 (dāna˚), Pv\-a.26 (left out in id. p. Kp\-a.209), Pv\-a.29, Pv\-a.65 (alabbhaneyya˚), Pv\-a.96 (id.), Pv\-a.119, Pv\-a.121 (iṭṭha˚), Pv\-a.177, Pv\-a.220. Cp. ˚bhūta.
3. occasion for, reason, ground AN.ii.158 (\+ khetta [in fig. sense!], āyatana & adhikaraṇa); AN.iv.334; DN.i.13 sq. (aṭṭhādasahi vatthūhi etc.) Ja.ii.5 (avatthumhi chandaṃ mâkari do not set your heart on what is unreasonable); *vatthunā* (instr.) because Pv\-a.118; *vatthuto* (abl.) on account of Pv\-a.241.
4. basis, foundation, seat, (objective) substratum, substance, element Ja.i.146 (kāyo paridevānaṃ v.); Vb\-a.404 (\+ ārammaṇa). See most of the compounds
5. subject\-matter, subject, story, account Snp\-a.4; Dhp\-a.ii.66; Pv\-a.77, Pv\-a.92, Pv\-a.263, Pv\-a.269. Cp. ˚gāthā & titles like Petavatthu, Vimānavatthu.

*\-[kata](kata.md)* made a foundation or basis of, practised thoroughly Ja.ii.61; Ja.v.14 and passim (\+ bhāvita etc.). In phrase *[tālāvatthukata](tālāvatthukata.md)* (= tāla avatthu kata) vatthu means foundation, basis, ground to feed and live on, thus “a palm deprived of its foundation”: see refs. under *tāla  
■ gāthā* the stanzas of the story, the introductory (explanatory, essential to its understanding) stanzas something like “prologue” Snp\-a.483, Snp\-a.575 (preceding Snp.699 & Snp.976); *\-[dasaka](dasaka.md)* tenfold substance or material basis Vb\-a.22. *\-[bhūta](bhūta.md)* being an object, i.e. subject to Ja.v.210. *\-[rūpa](rūpa.md)* substance or substratum of matter material form Vism.561, Vism.564; Vb\-a.22, Vb\-a.172. *\-[visadakiriyā](visadakiriyā.md)* clearing of the foundation or fundamentals purification of the elements Vb\-a.283 = Dhs\-a.76 (˚kiriyatā; translation *Expos.* 101 “cleansing of things or substance”); Vism.128; Vb\-a.276.

Class. Sk. vastu, fr. *vas1*

Vatthu2

site, ground, field, plot Vin.iii.50 (ārāma˚ & vihāra˚), Vin.iii.90 (id.); Snp.209, Snp.473 (sakhetta˚, cp. vatthu1 4), Snp.769 (khetta \+), Snp.858 (id.); Thag.957 (khetta \+ vatthu, cp. *Brethren* p. 3371 & *Vin Texts* iii.389 sq.); Mil.279 (khetta˚ a plot of arable land); DN\-a.i.78 (contrasted with khetta, see *[khetta](khetta.md)* 1 and cp. vatthu1 1; Pv\-a.88 (gehassa the back yard of the house); haunted by fairies (parigaṇhanti) DN.ii.87.

* *\-[kamma](kamma.md)* “act concerning sites,” i.e. preparing the ground for building DN.i.12 (translation: fixing on lucky sites for dwellings), cp. DN\-a.i.98: akaṭa\-vatthumhi gehapatiṭṭhāpanaṃ
* *\-[devatā](devatā.md)* the gods protecting the grounds field\-gods, house\-gods Pv.i.4#1 (= ghara\-vatthuṃ adhivatthā devatā Pv\-a.17).
* *\-[parikiraṇa](parikiraṇa.md)* offerings over the site of a house (“consecrating sites” translation) DN.i.12 (cp DN\-a.i.98 = balikamma\-karaṇaṃ).
* *\-[vijjā](vijjā.md)* the science of (building\-) sites, the art of determining a suitable (i.e. lucky) site for a house DN.i.9 (see expln at DN\-a.i.93) SN.iii.239; Mnd.372; Vism.269 (in comparison); Kp\-a.237 See also *Dial* ii.92 & Fick, *Sociale Gliederung* 152.
