Sineha & sneha

Both forms occur without distinction; *[sneha](sneha.md)* more frequently (as *archaic*) in poetry.

1. *[sineha](sineha.md)*:
   1. viscous liquid, unctuous moisture, sap SN.i.134; AN.i.223 sq.; Ja.i.108; Dhs.652 (= sinehana Dhs\-a.335); Vism.262 (thīna˚ = meda; vilīna˚ = vasā)
   2. fat Ja.ii.44 (bahu˚); Vb\-a.67.
   3. affection, love desire, lust Ja.i.190; Ja.ii.27; Pv\-a.82

2. *[sneha](sneha.md)*:
   1. (oily liquid) DN.i.74; Pv.iii.5#2 (anguṭṭha˚, something like milk explained as *[khīra](khīra.md)* Pv\-a.198).
   2. (affection) AN.ii.10; SN.iv.188 (kāma˚); Snp.36, Snp.209, Snp.943 (= chanda, pema rāga, Mnd.426); Ja.iv.11.

* *\-[anvaya](anvaya.md)* following an affection Snp.36.
* *\-[gata](gata.md)* anything moist or oily AN.iii.394 sq.; Dhs\-a.335.
* *\-[ja](ja.md)* sprung from affection Snp.272; SN.i.207.
* *\-[bindu](bindu.md)* a drop of oil Vism.263.
* *\-[virecana](virecana.md)* an oily purgative Ja.iii.48.
