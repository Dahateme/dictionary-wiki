(\~ā & \-ikā)n. & neuter

1. (mfn.) small; minor; insignificant; inferior; short.
2. (nom. pl. *\~ā* and *\~āni*), a minor rule or offense (i.e. a pācittiya rule).

= khudda; usually in compounds In sequence khuddaka\-majjhima\-mahā Vism.100. Of smaller sections or subdivisions of canonical books Vin.v.145 sq (with ref. to the paññattis), see also below  
■ catuppade kh˚ ca mahallake Snp.603. Khuddaka (m.) the little one, Mil.40 (mātā ˚assa).

* *\-[nadī](nadī.md)* = kunnadī, a small river Pv\-a.154;
* *\-[nikāya](nikāya.md)* name of a collection of canonical books, mostly short (the fifth of the five Nikāyas) comprising the foll. 15 books: Khuddaka\-Pāṭha, Dhammapada, Udāna, Itivuttaka Sutta\-Nipāta, Vimāna\-Vatthu, Peta\-Vatthu Thera and Therī Gāthā, Jātaka (verses only), Niddesa Paṭisambhidāmagga, Apadāna, Buddha\-Vaṃsa, CariyāPiṭaka. The name Kh\-N. is taken from the fact that it is a collection of short books\-short, that is, as compared with the Four Nikāyas. Anvs (*J.P.T.S.* 1886) p. 35 Gvns (*J.P.T.S.* 1886) p. 57; Pv\-a.2, etc.
* *\-[pāṭha](pāṭha.md)* Name of the first book in the Khuddaka Nikāya;
* *\-[mañcaka](mañcaka.md)* a small or low bed Ja.i.167;
* *\-[rājā](rājā.md)* an inferior king Ja.v.37 (\+ mahārājā) Snp\-a.121; cp. khujja & kuṭṭa;
* *\-vaggulī* (f.) a small singing bird Dhp\-a.iii.223;
