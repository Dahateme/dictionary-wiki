cloud Pv.ii.9#45; Vism.126; esp. a thundercloud storm, SN.i.100 (thaneti), SN.i.154; Thag.307 (as kāḷa) Iti.66; Ja.i.332 (pajjunna vuccati megha); Dhp\-a.i.19; Snp\-a.27 (˚thanita\-sadda). In this capacity often called *mahā\-megha*, e.g. Snp.30; Dhp\-a.i.165; Kp\-a.21; Pv\-a.132  
■ On megha in similes see *J.P.T.S.* 1907 124, 125.

* *\-[nātha](nātha.md)* having clouds as protectors (said with ref. to grass\-eating animals) Ja.iv.253.
* *\-[maṇḍala](maṇḍala.md)* cloud\-circle a circle of clouds Snp\-a.27.
* *\-[vaṇṇa](vaṇṇa.md)* cloud\-coloured Ja.v.321 (C. for megha\-sannibha); *˚pāsāṇa* a sort of ornamental building stone Mhvs.30, Mhvs.59 (variant reading, T. meda˚ trsl. fat\-coloured stones). See meda˚.

Vedic megha; *not* to *mih*, mehati (see *[mīḷha](mīḷha.md)*), but to Idg. *\*meigh\-*, fog, rain; cp. Sk. miḥ mist; Av maēga cloud; Gr. ὀμίξλη fog, Lith. mighá fog, Dutch miggelen to drizzle, also Ags. mist = Oicel mistr “mist”
