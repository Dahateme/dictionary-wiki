Nigha1

(nīgha) adjective noun is invented by Com. & scholiasts to explain the combn *[anigha](anigha.md)* (anīgha sporadic, e.g. SN.v.57). But this should be divided *an\-īgha* instead of *a\-nīgha*  
■ (m.) rage, trembling, confusion, only in formula rāgo n. doso n. moho n. explaining the adj anīgha. Thus at SN.iv.292 = Cnd.45; SN.v.57  
■ (adj. *[anigha](anigha.md)* not trembling, undisturbed, calm [nirāsa](nirāsa.md)*: SN.i.12 = SN.i.23, SN.i.141; Snp.1048, Snp.1060, Snp.1078. Explained correctly at Snp\-a.590 by rāgādi\-īgha\-virahita. Spelling *[anīgha](anīgha.md)* Ja.iii.443 (Com. niddukkha); Pv.iv.1#34 (\+ nirāsa explained by niddukkha Pv\-a.230). anīgha also at Iti.97 (\+ chinnasaṃsaya); Ud.76; Dhp.295 (variant reading aniggha explained by niddukkha Dhp\-a.iii.454).
