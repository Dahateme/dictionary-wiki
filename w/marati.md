inf *[marituṃ](marituṃ.md)* DN.ii.330 (amaritu\-kāma not willing to die); Vism.297 (id.); Vv\-a.207 (positive); and *marituye* Thig.426. The form *[miyyati](miyyati.md)* (mīyati) see separately  
caus 1 *[māreti](māreti.md)* to kill, murder Mhvs.37, Mhvs.27; Pv\-a.4. Pass *māriyati* Pv\-a.5 (ppr. māriyamāna); Sdhp.139 (read mār˚ for marīy˚)  
caus 2 *[mārāpeti](mārāpeti.md)* to cause to be killed Ja.iii.178; Mhvs.37, Mhvs.28. Cp. *[pamāreti](pamāreti.md)*.

*mṛ* = Idg. *\*mer*, Vedic mriyate & marate; cp. Av. miryeite, Sk. marta = Gr. βροτός mortal, man; māra death; Goth. maurpr = Ags. mort = Ger. mord; Lith mir̃ti to die; Lat. morior to die, mors death. The root is identical with that of mṛṇāti to crush: see maṇāti and mṛdnāti (mardati) same: see *[mattikā](mattikā.md)*  
■ The Dhtp (No. 245) defines *mṛ*; by “pāṇa\-cāge,” i.e. giving up breathing.
