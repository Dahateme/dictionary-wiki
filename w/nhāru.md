Nahāru & Nhāru

sinew, tendon muscle. In the anatomy of the body n. occupies the place between *[maṃsa](maṃsa.md)* (flesh, soft flesh) & *[aṭṭhi](aṭṭhi.md)* (bone) as is seen from ster. sequence chavi, camma, maṃsa nahāru, aṭṭhi, aṭṭhi\-miñja (e.g. at Vin.i.25; Ja.iii.84) See also defn in detail at Snp\-a.246 sq. & Kp\-a.47.; Vin.i.25 (nh˚); MN.i.429 (used for bow strings); AN.i.50 AN.iii.324; AN.iv.47 sq. (˚daddula), AN.129; Kp.111.; Snp.194 (aṭṭhi˚) Cnd.97 (nh˚); Dhp\-a.iii.118; Thag\-a.257 (nh˚) Pv\-a.68 (aṭṭhi\-camma˚), Pv\-a.80 (camma\-maṃsa˚); Sdhp.46 Sdhp.103.
