scum, foam, froth, only in compounds viz.:

* *\-[uddehakaṃ](uddehakaṃ.md)* (adv.) (paccamāna, boiling) with scum on top, throwing up foam MN.iii.167; AN.i.141; Cnd.304#iii c; Ja.iii.46; Mil.357.
* *\-[paṭala](paṭala.md)* a film of scum Vism.359; Vb\-a.65.
* *\-[piṇḍa](piṇḍa.md)* a lump or heap of foam SN.iii.140 sq. = Vism.479 (in simile of rūpa); Cnd.680 Aii Vism.40 (in comp); Vb\-a.32 sq. *[bubbuḷaka](bubbuḷaka.md)* a bubble of scum Vism.171, Vism.259, Vism.345; Vb\-a.242.
* *\-[mālā](mālā.md)* a wreath or garland of scum Mil.117.
* *\-[mālin](mālin.md)* with a wreath of scum Mil.260.
* *\-[missa](missa.md)* mixed with froth Vism.263.
* *\-[vaṇṇa](vaṇṇa.md)* colour of scum Vism.263.

cp. Vedic phena, with \*ph fr. sp˚, connected with Lat. spūma, scum, Ags. fām = Ger. feim = E. foam.
