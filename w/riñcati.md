to leave, abandon, leave behind, give up, neglect Vin.i.190 (see [fut riñcissati](fut riñcissati.md)); MN.i.155 (riñcissati), MN.i.403; SN.iv.206; AN.iii.86 sq., AN.iii.108 sq., AN.iii.343 sq., AN.iii.366 sq., AN.iii.437; Thag.1052; Snp.156; Mil.419; Ja.v.403  
ppr med. with neg.: *[ariñcamāna](ariñcamāna.md)* Snp.69; ger. *[riñcitvā](riñcitvā.md)* (for Sk. riktvā) Thig.93  
pp *[ritta](ritta.md)* \-Pass. *riccati* [Sk. ricyate] to be left: see *[ati](ati.md)*˚.

*ric*, in Vedic & Sk. rinakti; cp. Av. irinaxti to leave; Gr. λείπω id., λοιπός left; Lat. linquo id. Goth. leihwan = Ohg. līhan to lend; Ags lāēn = loan cp. E. leave etc  
■ The defn of the root at Dhtp is given in two forms, viz. *ric* as “virecane” (No. 396; cp Dhtm.517 “kharaṇe,” i.e. flowing; 610 “recane”) and *riñc* as “riñcane” (No. 44)
