twig, rod; creeper; junglerope (cp. veṇu\-daṇḍa); cane (calamus). By itself only in standard list of punishments (tortures): *vettehi tāḷeti* to flog with canes, e.g. AN.i.47; AN.ii.122; Mil.196 Otherwise freq. in compounds:

* *\-[agga](agga.md)* cane\-top, sprout of bamboo (cp. kaḷīra) Vism.255 (where Kp\-a in id. p. reads ˚ankura); Vb\-a.60, Vb\-a.239 Vb\-a.252.
* *\-[aṅkura](aṅkura.md)* a shoot of bamboo Kp\-a.52, Kp\-a.67.
* *\-[āsana](āsana.md)* cane chair Vv\-a.8.
* *\-[cāra](cāra.md)* (vettācāra) “stick\-wandering” (?) Ja.iii.541 (\+ sankupatha; C.: vettehi sañcaritabba); Vv.84#11 (vettācāraṃ sankupathañ ca maggaṃ explained as vettalatā bandhitvā ācaritabba magga Vv\-a.338); better as “jungle\-path.”
* *\-[patha](patha.md)* “a jungle full of sticks” (translation Rh. D.) Mil.280 (\+ sankupatha) jungle\-path.
* *\-[bandhana](bandhana.md)* binding with twigs (rope?) creeper\-bands SN.iii.155; SN.v.51 = AN.iv.127.
* *\-[latā](latā.md)* cane creeper Ja.i.342; Vv\-a.8, Vv\-a.338.
* *\-[valli](valli.md)* garland of creeper Dāvs iii.40.

cp. Epic Sk. vetra
