Susu1

a boy, youngster, lad Vin.iii.147 = Ja.ii.284; Vv.64#14 (= dahara C.); Snp.420; DN.i.115; MN.i.82; AN.ii.22; Ja.ii.57; ājānīya\-susūpama MN.i.445 read ājānīy\-ass\-ūpama (cp. Thag.72)  
■ In phrase *susukāḷa* the susu is a *double* su˚, in meaning “very, very black” (see under *[kāḷa](kāḷa.md)*\-kesa), e.g. DN.i.115 = MN.i.82; AN.ii.22 = AN.iii.66 = Ja.ii.57; explained as suṭṭhu\-kāḷa DN\-a.i.284\- *susunāga* a young elephant DN.ii.254.

cp. Sk. śiśu

Susu3
