ladle, a spoon; explained by uḷunka Dhp\-a.iv.75, Dhp\-a.iv.123; by dabbi Pv\-a.135. Used for butter Vv\-a.68, otherwise for cooked food in general, esp. rice gruel  
■ Vin.ii.216; Ja.i.454; Ja.iii.277.

* *\-[gāha](gāha.md)* “holding on to one’s spoon,” i.e. disinclination to give food, niggardliness, stinginess Dhs\-a.376 cp. Dhs trsl.300 n2.
* *\-[gāhika](gāhika.md)* “spoon in hand,” serving with ladles (in the distribution of food at the Mahādāna Pv\-a.135.
* *\-[parissāvana](parissāvana.md)* a perforated ladle Vin.ii.118
* *\-[bhikkhā](bhikkhā.md)* “ladle\-begging,” i.e. the food given with a ladle to a bhikkhu when he calls at a house on his begging tour Thag.934; Mil.9; Dhp\-a.iv.123; as representing a small gift to one individual, opposed to the Mahādanā Pv.ii.9#57; as an individual meal contrasted with public feeding (salāka\-bhatta) Dhp\-a.i.379.
* *\-[matta](matta.md)* (bhatta “only a spoonful of rice” Mil.8; Dhp\-a.iv.75.

cp. on etym. Morris in *J.P.T.S.* 1887, 163
