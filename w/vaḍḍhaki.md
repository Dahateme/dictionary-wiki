(& *\-ī*) a carpenter builder, architect, mason. On their craft and guilds see Fick, *Sociale Gliederung* 181 sq.; Mrs. Rh. D *Cambridge Hist. Ind.* i.206  
■ The word is specially characteristic of the Jātakas and other popular (later literature Ja.i.32, Ja.i.201, Ja.i.247; Ja.ii.170; Ja.vi.332 sq., Ja.vi.432 Tha\-ap.51; Dhp\-a.i.269; Dhp\-a.iv.207; Vism.94; Pv\-a.141; Mhbv.154  
■ *iṭṭha˚*; a stonemason Mhvs.35, Mhvs.102; *nagara˚*; the city architect Mil.331, Mil.345; *brāhmaṇa˚*; a brahmin carpenter Ja.iv.207; *mahā˚*; chief carpenter, master builder Vism.463. In metaphor *[taṇhā](taṇhā.md)* the artificer lust Dhp\-a.iii.128.

* *\-[gāma](gāma.md)* a carpenter village Ja.ii.18, Ja.ii.405; Ja.iv.159.

cp. Epic & Class. Sk. vardhaki & vardhakin; perhaps from *vardh* to cut: see *[vaddheti](vaddheti.md)*
