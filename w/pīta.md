2. soaked or saturated with (\-˚), in *kasāyarasa˚*; Ja.ii.98 (or = pīta2?) and *[visapīta](visapīta.md)* (of an arrow) Ja.v.36; Vism.303, Vism.381; which may however be read (on acct. of variant reading visappīta) as *visappita* “poison\-applied” (see *[appita](appita.md)*) Does MN.i.281 pīta\-nisita belong here (= visapīta)?

■ *[pīta](pīta.md)* is prominent (in the sense of golden) in the description of Vimānas or other heavenly abodes. A typical example is Vv.47 (Pītavimāna v.1 & 2), where everything is characterised as pīta, viz. vattha, dhaja, alankāra, candana, uppala pāsāda, āsana, bhojana, chatta, ratha, assa, bījanī the C. expln of pīta at this passage is “suvaṇṇa”; cp Vv.36#1 (= parisuddha, hemamaya Vv\-a.166); Vv.78#4 (= suvaṇṇamaya C. 304).
